# Aisha — round 2

## Round-1 → round-2 movement
Round 1: **7**. Round 2: **8**. Up one point. The two things that most held me off in round 1 are genuinely fixed; the third is sidestepped rather than fixed.

### Were my round-1 blockers fixed?
1. **Differentiator buried behind per-row carets (read like editable selects)** — FIXED. There's now a "YOUR CHART, IN PLAIN ENGLISH" section surfaced by DEFAULT, above the houses table. Every placement (Pisces Sun · House 10 · "Core identity", etc.) gets a full plain-English paragraph with zero clicking. This is the right call and it's what sells the app's whole premise. The differentiator is no longer hidden.
2. **Cryptic geocoder labels (raw region codes)** — FIXED. Typing "Lon" returns "London, United Kingdom" (famous one first), "Long Beach, United States", "London, Canada" — readable country names, clean row spacing, good hit targets. The selected place even confirms coords ("51.5085, -0.1257"). Considered.
3. **Expanded-cell layout breaks grid rhythm (narrow sliver)** — NOT fixed at the table, but now moot-ish. The inline table expansions (clicking a sign/planet pill) STILL render into a cramped column-width sliver — Scorpio's reading wraps into a ~50px column, Uranus into ~120px. The "full-width on desktop" fix only applies to the new top section, not the table. BUT since the top section already gives me every reading full-width, I almost never need the table expansions now, so it stops being a dealbreaker.

## 1. ADVOCACY: 8/10
The plain-English-first reframe is exactly the design instinct I'd advocate for — explanation over an inscrutable wheel, a clean houses TABLE with theme labels (Self & identity, Money & values), a real Element Distribution bar (4 Fire / 5 Earth / 1 Air / 1 Water), and a "Today's Sky" transit card with a "1 planet retrograde" callout and natal-to-transit plain readings. That's a considered, legible product I'd send to my astrology-curious friends. Two things keep it off a 9: (a) the table's inline pill-expansions still break into a narrow sliver — a craft inconsistency a designer notices immediately, and the carets still read like dropdowns; (b) template seams show — "Precise and helpful, you notice what others miss…" is copy-pasted verbatim under Virgo Rising, Virgo Mercury AND Virgo Venus, which undercuts the "personal reading" feel.

## 2. VALUE: Yes
I read about my chart for fun and bounce off astro-seek's dense wheels. This gives me my own chart computed on-device, no signup, with a genuinely readable narrative plus a daily-transit layer — that's more than I get from the free tools I use today.

## 3. CLARITY: Yes
Headline "Your birth chart, explained in plain English." + "Free, no signup" subhead + a "Load example (Einstein)" button told me what it does and how to start within ~5 seconds. Empty state ("Your explained chart will appear here") is tidy.

## Single biggest remaining gap
Table inline-expansion still renders in a cramped column sliver (grid rhythm breaks) AND repeated boilerplate copy across same-sign placements — both are craft/polish, not function.
