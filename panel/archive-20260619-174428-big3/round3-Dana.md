# Dana — Round 3

Demand-gen marketer, medium-tech, curious-about-astrology, ruthless about time. Round 3 — re-testing the synastry / "Compare two people" feature after a fix.

## Re-check of my Round 2 complaints
- **Long-tail "Show all 50" was 40/50 boilerplate (5 canned lines)** — FIXED. Expanded the tail: 88 descriptive lines, 85 unique. Only 3 lines repeat (each just 2x, and they're real planet-pair readings like "Jupiter conjunct Venus…", not the old generic templates). Every one of the 50 aspect headers names BOTH people directionally ("Albert Einstein's Neptune □ Michelle Obama's Mars"). The wall is gone.
- **No "Show fewer" to re-collapse** — FIXED. There's now a "SHOW TOP ASPECTS ONLY" button; clicking it collapses back to the top 6 and restores the "SHOW ALL 50 ASPECTS" toggle. Works on desktop AND mobile.
- **Mobile auto-scroll overshot the header onto element bars** — LARGELY FIXED. On 375px, tapping the compare card now lands on "COMPATIBILITY SUMMARY" with PERSON A / PERSON B big-three side-by-side at the top of the viewport (the screenshot-worthy bit). It sits just below the "Compatibility, explained" header rather than mid-element-bars — a clear improvement.
- **Compare hidden until you compute a chart** — STILL NOT addressed. The cold home page (desktop and 375px) shows ONLY the natal form; there is no "Compatibility / Compare two people" entry above the fold, no /compare route. The compare CARD only appears AFTER you compute or load a chart. A synastry-seeker arriving cold still sees a birth-chart form and could bounce in one scroll.

## The three questions
1. **What worked / value** — The compatibility view is genuinely strong now. The card is a clear clickable CARD ("COMPATIBILITY" eyebrow, "Compare two people", working "→"); clicking opens "Compatibility, explained" with a "← BACK TO CHART". Big-three side-by-side, per-person element bars, and an HONEST "30 HARMONY · 15 TENSION · 5 CONJUNCTION" summary with NO fake % match. Top KEY ASPECTS are the relationship-significant ones (Sun⚹Sun, Mars☌Sun at 0.2° orb, Moon△Jupiter), each fully directional with both names. House overlays read with correct ordinals ("1st house", "8th house", "7th house") — no "1 house" bug. The "SHARE THIS COMPARISON" button works: POSTs to /api/chart-share and copies a real link (e.g. /chart/HxOMC1XCFT8ly4t0U0zgQmrt); I reopened it in a clean browser (no localStorage, simulating a friend) and it rebuilt the FULL compatibility view — "Albert Einstein × Michelle Obama", both big-threes, element bars, harmony/tension count, KEY ASPECTS, plus a "Create your own chart →" CTA. That shared page is exactly the clean free explainer I'd drop in the team channel. No horizontal clipping at 375px before or after expanding; natal chart still fully works (big-three, 11 placements, retrograde markers). Zero console errors throughout.
2. **What frustrated / felt broken** — Mainly discoverability: compatibility is invisible on arrival. If I land cold wanting to compare two people, the home page only offers a single birth-chart form — I have to first compute SOMEONE'S chart before I even learn the app does synastry. (Tester note: my first share-button click hit the `<p class="ds-eyebrow">` heading, not the `<button>`, so it looked dead — that was MY mis-click; the actual button works perfectly.) Minor: comparing requires two SAVED charts, so a first-timer must enter/compute two people before they get any comparison — a small barrier for the screenshot-and-go use case.
3. **Use again / recommend** — Yes. I'd save my partner's + a coworker's chart and screenshot the top aspects + harmony/tension count, or just send the share link. Today I'd screenshot from Co–Star or a free astro-seek synastry table; this is cleaner, plain-English, directional, and shareable with one link.

## Scores
- **Advocacy: 8/10** (same as R2, but for better reasons). The three things I dinged the feature itself for — boilerplate tail, no re-collapse, mobile overshoot — are all fixed, and the share link is a real, working, screenshot-class artifact. Held back from 9 ONLY by discoverability: the feature they polished is still hidden behind computing a chart, so a synastry-seeker doesn't see it in one scroll. Put a "Compare two people" entry above the fold on the home page and this is a 9.
- **Value: yes** — clear, real, and shareable; I'd use it weekly.
- **Clarity: partially** — the natal chart is instantly clear; compatibility is excellent ONCE you find it, but it's not discoverable in the first scroll, so a compatibility-seeker doesn't get clarity cold.

**Dominant note:** Every defect I flagged INSIDE the synastry feature in R2 is fixed — distinct named aspect readings, a working collapse, correct house ordinals, and a share link that reopens the full comparison for a friend. The only thing keeping it off a 9 is unchanged from R2: compare is buried until you compute a chart, so the strongest feature is invisible above the fold.
