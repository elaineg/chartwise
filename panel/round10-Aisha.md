Advocacy: 9/10 — the SSENSE reskin made an already-useful tool feel genuinely high-end; the element bars and houses table are the kind of monochrome data design I'd screenshot into a Figma moodboard.

## Re-check of my prior concern (the look)
Across rounds 1-9 my standing gripe was that the craft never quite matched the smart explanation-first idea. The pure visual reskin fixes that. Function/data/readings/chart accuracy are unchanged (Einstein chart identical placements, share link still works), so I judged the skin alone. Verdict: this reads as GENUINE austere editorial minimalism, not a half-baked imitation. Addressed: all (my concern was visual; it's resolved).

## Craft wins (specific)
- ELEMENT DISTRIBUTION is the hero. Solid black bars on a light-grey track, tabular numerals, tracked uppercase row labels (FIRE/EARTH/AIR/WATER), and a grey "Based on 11 placements:" footnote. This is the correct monochrome translation — length carries the data, no color crutch. Fully SSENSE.
- Houses table reads beautifully in mono: HOUSE/SIGN/PLANETS/NODES micro-labels with a single 1px header rule, bold house number + grey descriptor stack ("Self & identity / ASC: CANCER"), hairline-bordered value pills. The expandable reading drops into a light-grey fill block — a real editorial device, not a generic accordion.
- Hierarchy is right: big tight Helvetica-ish headline, grey kicker ("NATAL CHART · PLAIN ENGLISH · NO SIGNUP"), one filled-black primary button vs hairline-outlined secondary. Clear primary/secondary language done with weight + fill, not color.
- Whitespace rhythm is generous and confident; square corners executed cleanly everywhere I looked.
- Today's Sky cards: hairline borders, glyph + bold name + grey sign. Consistent with the system.
- Honest grey privacy line on share ("sends this chart's birth info to our server") — considered copy, not boilerplate.

## Craft misses (specific, minor)
- The hairline weight isn't perfectly uniform: the houses-table value-pill borders read slightly heavier/darker than the section divider rules and the Today's Sky card borders. A true SSENSE system uses ONE hairline token everywhere; here it's close but not pixel-identical. Tighten to a single border color/weight.
- The required-field asterisks (BIRTH DATE *, BIRTH PLACE *) are RED — the only color in an otherwise zero-color palette. In a strict ink/paper system that red is a tell. Make them ink or grey.
- "City not found? Enter coordinates manually" and the "Unknown" time checkbox label are sentence-case while everything else structural is tracked-uppercase micro-label — small inconsistency in the form's label language.
- Empty state ("Your explained chart will appear here / Enter your birth data or load the Einstein example") is fine but flat; an austere system can afford a touch more intent here (it's the one screen a first-timer stares at). Not broken, just the least-considered surface.
- Retro-bright icon glyphs for planets render a hair small/light next to the bold names — slightly weak contrast in the transit cards.

## Why 9 not 10
It's the most considered version of this app I've seen and I'd bring it up unprompted to design-leaning friends. Holding back one point for the non-uniform hairline weight and the stray red asterisks — in a system this committed to monochrome, those are exactly the details a craft-judge eye snags on. Fix those two and it's a 10.
