Re-tested on desktop (1280px, Edge-equivalent). Loaded Einstein example, read houses table,

## Prior concerns re-check
- "Element distribution unreadable as a chart" (my long-standing gripe): **FIXED.** It's now a
  proper horizontal bar chart — Earth 5 / Fire 4 / Air 1 / Water 1, sorted dominant-first, bar
  length = count, Earth's bar darker + taller with a bigger numeral. I read the balance in one
  glance without touching the numbers. This is exactly how I'd build it in Tableau.
- Privacy honesty: still good. "computed on your device — nothing is sent anywhere," and the
  share box explicitly warns that creating a link DOES send birth info to the server. As a
  fine-print reader, I respect that it's stated plainly, not buried.

## (a) Element distribution comparable AT A GLANCE? — YES
Bar lengths track the counts, dominant row is visually heaviest, grey ramp reinforces rank.
No number-reading required. Best part of the app for someone like me.

## (b) Monochrome SSENSE look — appealing / legible / trustworthy? — YES on all three
High contrast, generous whitespace, real typography hierarchy. Critically for a skeptic: it
does NOT look like a purple-stars psychic-hotline site, so it reads as a serious tool, which
actually *raises* my trust. Legible on a corporate laptop. No console errors anywhere; share
link returned HTTP 200 in a fresh tab. COPY LINK populated the clipboard fine.
