# Round 1 — Rob (Freelance brand/visual designer; casual curious astrology skeptic; desktop)

## 1) Advocacy: 6/10 — would I actually tell a friend? Only sometimes.
The headline "Your birth chart, explained in plain English" plus the instant "big three"
pills (Libra rising / Virgo Sun / Virgo Moon) are genuinely a good party-share hook — that's
the one thing a non-believer like me actually wants. The "Free, no signup" promise held: I
typed my own data, the city autocompleted with coords, and my chart popped instantly with
nothing to send. I'd forward THE PILLS to a friend who's into this.

What kills the 9-10: the moment I scroll past the pills it becomes a 12-row HOUSE / SIGN /
PLANETS / NODES table — exactly the dense reference-manual look that makes me bounce. The
fun, legible plain-English sentences (e.g. Sun = "Your life force goes into career and public
standing; achievement and recognition feel essential, not optional") are HIDDEN behind
clicking each dropdown chip. I'd never click 30 chips. The good copy is buried under a grid.

## 2) Value: No (for me as a non-believer).
What I'd do today: nothing, or glance at a free Co-Star/cafeastrology page. This computes my
big three faster than those and with no signup, which is nice — but once I have the three
words, the table gives me no reason to stay. It's not solving a recurring problem for me; it's
a one-time novelty. A believer might re-check transits, but I won't.

## 3) Clarity: Yes.
Within ~5 seconds I knew exactly what it is and how to use it: title + subtitle + a big
"Load example (Einstein)" button. Zero confusion about the purpose or the input form.

## Concrete likes
- Crisp, honest value prop; "Free, no signup" stated upfront and true.
- Big-three pills are the right hero element for a casual user.
- Instant on-device compute; clean city autocomplete with coordinate confirmation.
- Element Distribution bar is a nice visual a designer appreciates; "Today's Sky" is fun.

## Concrete defects (density / legibility)
- DENSITY: default chart view is a 12-row 4-column data table — reads as a reference manual,
  not a fun explainer. The headline promises "plain English" but the default is a spreadsheet.
- BURIED VALUE: the actual plain-English sentences only appear after clicking each planet/sign
  dropdown one by one. No "explain the whole thing in a paragraph" or expand-all. I won't dig.
- JARGON: "FOR YOUR CHART" transit lines read like a mad-lib — "Mars in Taurus is visiting
  your natal Neptune's sign — themes of mars and neptune blend right now" (lowercase planet
  names, vague "themes blend"). Meaningless to a non-believer.
- No quick summary of what my big three MEAN in one place; pills tease but don't pay off
  without hunting through the grid.
